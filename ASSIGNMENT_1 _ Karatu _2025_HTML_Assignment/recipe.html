Classic Spaghetti Carbonara

This traditional Italian pasta dish is made with eggs, cheese, pancetta, and pepper. It’s rich, creamy, and comforting — the perfect meal for pasta lovers. No cream required!

Recipe Information

Prep Time: 10 minutes

Cook Time: 15 minutes

Servings: 4 people

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta (or guanciale), diced
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1 large egg yolk
  • 100g grated Parmesan or Pecorino Romano
  • 2 cloves garlic, peeled
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Freshly ground black pepper (to taste)
  • Salt (for pasta water)

Instructions

  1. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil and cook spaghetti until al dente.
  2. While pasta cooks, heat olive oil in a pan and sauté garlic cloves. Add pancetta and cook until crispy. Remove garlic.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together eggs, egg yolk, and grated cheese until smooth.
  4. Drain spaghetti, reserving ½ cup of pasta water. Quickly toss pasta with pancetta in the pan.
  5. Remove from heat, then stir in egg-cheese mixture, adding a splash of pasta water to create a creamy sauce.
  6. Season generously with black pepper and serve immediately with extra cheese.

Tips & Notes

Make sure to remove the pan from heat before adding the egg mixture, otherwise the eggs may scramble. Use guanciale for the most authentic flavor.
